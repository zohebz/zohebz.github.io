Robotics with LEGO NXT
Designed and made a 4-wheeled fighting robot for Robo War competition at NIT, Calicut. The robot consists of 4 dc motors, 1 for each wheel and a flipper powered by two high torque dc motors. Also have Practical knowledge in designing Robots and programing them using software like ROS (Robot Operating Sytem), GAZEBO Simulator, LEGO NXT and Arduino and Delivered a talk as a resource person on building and programing a robot using LEGO NXT at an IEEE student workshop at TKMCE.


Obsticle Avoidanve and Terrain mapping in Tortoise Bot
To cultivate my proficiency in ROS, I constructed and configured a ROS-programmed tortoise bot, employing Python, GAZEBO simulations, and Ubuntu Linux to establish a comprehensive repository of the project. This experience not only fortified my ROS and programming skills but also showcased my adeptness in designing and simulating robotic systems.


Force feedback measurement on Joysticks
I reengineered joystick hardware to integrate Arduino-controlled force feedback and leveraged built-in accelerometers for acceleration data. This project, programmed using Arduino and Python, culminated in a comprehensive GitHub repository (https://github.com/zohebz/zohebz.github.io), showcasing my hands-on technical skills and collaborative coding practices.
